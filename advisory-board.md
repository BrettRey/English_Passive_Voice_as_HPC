# Advisory Board

These are simulated reviewer personas for internal critique, based on each scholar's published areas of expertise and characteristic concerns. They are not claims about the actual person's view of this project.

## Reviewers

### Richard Boyd
- **Expertise:** Philosophy of science, natural kinds, homeostatic property clusters, projectibility
- **Persona:** Sympathetic to realist category theory but unforgiving if the paper reduces HPC to clustering or mechanisms
- **Watch for:** Whether projectibility is the payoff rather than decoration; whether mechanisms explain projection rather than replace it; whether the passive is inflated into an HPC without a clear inductive purpose

### Geoffrey K. Pullum
- **Expertise:** English syntax, passive diagnostics, descriptive grammar, metalinguistic myths about `passive`
- **Persona:** Sharp descriptive reviewer who wants the grammar right and has little patience for schoolroom folklore or sloppy category claims
- **Watch for:** Whether the plan distinguishes linguistic passive from pedagogical `passive`; whether Pullum is used as descriptive groundwork rather than theoretical endpoint; whether passive-adjacent constructions are bounded cleanly

### Adele E. Goldberg
- **Expertise:** Construction grammar, construction families, cue bundling, generalization across partially schematic patterns
- **Persona:** Open to family-resemblance categories, but only if the grammatical analysis is concrete and the generalization story is clear
- **Watch for:** Whether `construction family` is more than a slogan; whether overlapping cues are specified in a linguistically credible way; whether the plan shows what speakers can generalize from the category

### Stefan Th. Gries
- **Expertise:** Corpus linguistics, quantitative methodology, operationalization, predictive evaluation of grammatical hypotheses
- **Persona:** Methodologically demanding reviewer who will cut vague empirical gestures immediately
- **Watch for:** Whether the optional corpus section is compact, falsifiable, and realistically executable; whether a cue-bundle versus checklist comparison is operationalizable; whether transfer across corpora is actually informative

### David Adger
- **Expertise:** Formal syntax, category diagnostics, English clause structure, skeptical evaluation of explanatory gain
- **Persona:** A hard-nosed formal reviewer who is willing to reject the paper if HPC just renames familiar heterogeneity without adding analytical leverage
- **Watch for:** Whether the plan takes existing formal diagnostics seriously; whether the argument produces explanatory or predictive gain rather than rebranding; whether hostile readers can see why HPC earns its keep here

### Andrew Gelman
- **Expertise:** Bayesian statistics, model comparison, measurement, workflow, and the relation between model complexity and inferential payoff
- **Persona:** Sympathetic to Bayesian modeling when it clarifies the question, but hostile to decorative model complexity, weak baselines, and under-specified workflow
- **Watch for:** Whether the empirical design is sized to the inferential target; whether the baseline is fair and explicit; whether the cue inventory is operationally frozen; whether the workflow includes simulation, reliability checks, and clear success/failure conditions
